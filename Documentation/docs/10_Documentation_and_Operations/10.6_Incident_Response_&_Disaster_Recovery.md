# Purpose

This document defines the incident response framework, disaster recovery procedures, and business continuity strategy for **The Last-Minute Life Saver**.

Its goal is to minimize service disruption, protect user data, and restore production services quickly after unexpected events.

---

# Objectives

The strategy should ensure:

* Rapid incident detection
* Clear ownership
* Controlled incident response
* Minimal downtime
* Minimal data loss
* Reliable recovery
* Continuous business operations
* Continuous improvement

---

# Scope

This document covers:

* Service outages
* Security incidents
* Infrastructure failures
* Database failures
* AI provider outages
* Cloud service disruptions
* Network failures
* Regional disasters
* Human error
* Data corruption

# Manual Fallback Procedures

If AI services are unavailable:

* Use rule-based scheduling
* Continue task management
* Continue reminders
* Disable advanced AI coaching temporarily

Core productivity features must remain operational.

# Best Practices

* Detect incidents early through proactive monitoring.
* Automate recovery where practical.
* Test backups regularly by performing real restoration exercises.
* Practice disaster recovery scenarios before they occur.
* Keep communication timely and transparent.
* Treat every major incident as an opportunity to improve reliability.
* Review and update recovery procedures after infrastructure changes.
* Minimize manual recovery steps through automation.

---

# Antigravity Development Instructions

When implementing incident response and disaster recovery:

1. Define clear ownership for every incident severity level.
2. Maintain automated monitoring and alerting across all production services.
3. Protect critical systems with redundant infrastructure and verified backups.
4. Implement AI provider failover to preserve essential scheduling functionality.
5. Test recovery procedures regularly through backup restoration and disaster recovery exercises.
6. Record comprehensive post-incident reviews for every major event.
7. Continuously improve recovery procedures based on operational experience.
8. Maintain infrastructure as code to enable rapid environment recreation.
9. Ensure business continuity by prioritizing restoration of critical services first.
10. Review disaster recovery objectives annually and adjust them as the platform evolves.
