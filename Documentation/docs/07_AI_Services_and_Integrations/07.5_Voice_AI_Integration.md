# Purpose

The Voice AI Integration enables users to interact with the application using natural speech instead of traditional UI interactions.

Voice should be treated as an alternative interface—not a separate AI system.

The Voice Service converts speech into structured requests for the Multi-Agent AI architecture and converts AI responses back into speech.

---

# Objectives

The Voice System should:

* Support natural conversations
* Convert speech to text
* Convert text to speech
* Execute voice commands
* Support multilingual conversations
* Enable hands-free productivity
* Work with all AI agents
* Maintain conversation context

---

# Best Practices

* Keep responses concise when spoken.
* Always confirm destructive actions.
* Allow users to interrupt speech.
* Optimize for low latency.
* Maintain conversation context.
* Support multilingual conversations.
* Never block UI during voice processing.

---

# Antigravity Development Instructions

When implementing the Voice AI Integration:

1. Build a dedicated **Voice Service** between the UI and the AI Orchestrator.
2. Integrate Speech-to-Text (STT) for converting user speech into text with confidence scoring.
3. Implement a Voice Command Parser that extracts intents and entities before sending requests to the AI Orchestrator.
4. Use Text-to-Speech (TTS) to generate natural voice responses from AI outputs.
5. Support contextual conversations by maintaining a short-lived conversation memory separate from long-term AI memory.
6. Require confirmation for destructive or high-impact actions such as deleting tasks or overwriting schedules.
7. Support multilingual interactions beginning with English, Marathi, and Hindi.
8. Provide configurable voice settings, including language, voice, speed, and wake phrase.
9. Ensure privacy by minimizing voice data retention and encrypting temporary audio data.
10. Optimize the complete voice interaction pipeline for low latency and hands-free usability.
